Early Life

 Alan Wagner was born in New Orleans to parents Billiam Wagner and Loretta Wagner. His father, coal miner by trade, would perform with his jazz group, The Swinging Beboops, in the evenings at local jazz clubs. His parents were so heavly invested in hazz culture they did not notice their only child was more interested in the pragmatic beauty of numbers and accounting.

 After being rejected from business school due to his colorblindness, Alan started his pursuit into entertainment. He began by writing errotic puppet shows, mostly performing for eldery communities. His success in puppeteering lead him to meet his first manager, Larry 'Stache Man' Williams. Stache man encouraged Alan to start incorporating more comedy into his pieces as he thought mainstream media would find his work too obscene. Alan then enrolled in the Comedy Univeristy of America where he majored in Comedian studies.

Career

 After excelling at the Comedy Univeristy of America, Alan began posting his unique irreverent style to social media platforms online. He quickly rose to fame and began making appearances on talk shows such as The Ellen Degeneres Show and Live with Regis and Kelly. In 2013, Alan performed at the Just for Laughs comedy festival in Montreal, Canada.

 Alan has also starred as a background performer in many blockbuster hits including Holes, Avengers: Endgame and Barbie.

 In 2023, Alan released his first book titled Wagner Book: The Collected Work of Alan Wagner. The book rose to number 5 on the New York Times Best Seller list and won second place in Quality Paperback Nonfiction in the 32nd New York Book Show held by the The Book Industry Guild of New York. His second book, Wagner's Rip-Em-Outs was released in 2024. His second book included a collection of his signs on perforated paper.

Personal Life

 Alan has bumped knuckles with the likes of Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Tom Hanks, and former President, Barack Obama.
